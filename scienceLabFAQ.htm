Science Lab FAQ

Science Lab is a program started by Science Gurus to develop and encourage the interest in science among today’s youth. It involves youth members (high school and undergraduate students) to take initiative in teaching and inspiring 4th-10th grade students to love science.

  • What is the Science Lab Program?

    Science Lab is a program (offered by Science Gurus organization) outside the school to supplement the school’s science curriculum with hands-on activities and field trips

  • What is the format of the program?

    The program caters to elementary grade students and junior high school students.

    Elementary and Junior High Grade: 4th through 8th: Will have classes to learn simple science concepts with hands-on activities. This supplements the science topics learned in school.

    The program will be offered in two sessions (Fall and Spring), meeting during the school year. It consists of weekly meetings for 8 weeks split as follows:
    * 6 weeks of single 1.5-hour meeting per week involving simple science concepts with hands-on activities
    * 1 week of Science Fair preparation for school level
    * 1 week of field trip – this will be on a weekend with parent chaperones

    Junior High Grade: 7th and 8th: Prepare students with a Science Fair Project that can be presented at the Regional Science Fair.
    The program will pair an instructor with 1 or 2 students and help students prepare a high quality science fair project:
    * Start in Fall with students going through several ideas and narrowing down a topic for research
    * Help students formulate the question, design the experiments, gather data, analyze data, present the data in the correct format and do background research
    * Write up the project to present in Regional Science Fair (Synopsis, ACSEF, etc.) in March

  • Who are the instructors?

    High school upperclassmen and adult parents – all with a passion towards science and teaching.

  • What is the student-teacher ratio?

    5:1

  • Where will the classes be held?

    Science Lab instructors will go out to certain areas where a parent volunteer can gather 5 students to form a class. Parents interested in hosting classes can contact by email: sciencelab@ScienceGurus.org

  • Is there a fee for the classes?

    Yes, there is a nominal fee of $100 per team which will cover the cost of materials needed for hands-on activities.

  • What is the deadline to sign up a team of students?

    Deadline to sign up is 10th Sep. for Fall session and 25th Jan for Spring session.

  • How do I sign up to host?

    Please send email to sciencelab@ScienceGurus.org to volunteer to host.

  • What if I am unable to host, but would like my child to enroll?

    Please see if another parent from the same school area can volunteer to host. Once we have a host in a particular area, we can inform interested parents for enrollment. Without a host, the classes cannot be conducted.

  • What if I can only host some weeks, but not all 7 weeks?

    Parents can take turns in hosting, provided they live close by.

  • What do I do if I want to be an Instructor?

    If you are a high school student interested in becoming an Instructor, please send an email to sciencelab@ScienceGurus.org indicating your interest to teach with your resume. Instructors attend a two-week summer internship to get trained on the curriculum and how to teach a particular grade level.

  • Will students get a certificate upon completion of a session?

    Yes

  • What are the rewards for Instructors?

    Community service hours will be provided. High School students will be guided on science research and internship opportunities, and science fair projects. Reference letters will be provided.



Want to sign up to host or as an Instructor? Contact sciencelab@ScienceGurus.org
